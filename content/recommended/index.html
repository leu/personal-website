What I've Learned

Journalling

Writing daily in my journal is probably the most helpful thing I've learned to do. You can't really think without writing it down. It also really helps with going through with doing things differently in your life. I highly recommend trying it.

Avoid Social Media

I also recommend avoiding YouTube, Instagram, or whatever social media you use.
Pros:

  • It makes you happy at the very start.

Cons:

  • They are addictive meaning you spend the rest of the day on them.
  • Slight depression
    • Feeling like I could've done something better with my time
    • Comparing yourself to other people
  • Your perception of the world gets warped by the content you consume, and rather than coming to your own conclusions based on your own experiences, for a while you frame things around the ideas of the last thing you watched/read

Standing Desk

One more thing that is strangely effective is using a standing desk. It's hard to explain without trying it out yourself, but you become way more productive if you're standing. You will get tired at first - but these days I can stand the whole day without ever sitting!

Love is Wise

One last thing. It's good to have disagreements - this can drive learning. But in general, you'll probably be happier if you make an effort for the people in your life.
